Acknowledgements

Support, inspiration, and selected references for this project.

This project was developed with support from the CHI Fellowship, whose funding and encouragement made it possible to explore Chinese cultural motifs through digital design, ethnocomputing, and culturally responsive computing education.

I also want to thank the Culturally Situated Design Tools (CSDT) website and the broader ethnocomputing framework that informs it. This project builds on CSDT’s core idea that cultural practices can be understood as sites of mathematical, computational, and design knowledge, and that these traditions can be translated into meaningful learning experiences.

I am grateful as well to the mentors, researchers, and creators whose scholarship, media, and archival materials helped shape the historical, cultural, and pedagogical framing of this website.

Additional historical and visual references on cloud motifs, yunjian, Shanwen armor, and contemporary design adaptation were used throughout the site to support interpretation of Chinese cultural motifs across historical and modern contexts.
