The Four-Fold Cloud Pattern

Jiangnan Region • Origins: 14th Century • Symbolism: Auspiciousness

Historical Context

The Four-Fold Cloud pattern is deeply rooted in the textile and architectural history of the Jiangnan region (encompassing modern-day Jiangsu, Zhejiang, Shanghai, and Anhui). This motif is distinctly recognized by its four curling nodes that intersect at a central point.

Unlike earlier flowing cloud designs, the brutal symmetry of the four-fold variation emerged as a standardized pattern during the late Yuan and early Ming dynasties, heavily utilized in official brocades and structural woodcarvings.

Symbolic Meaning

Geometrically, the four folds represent the Four Cardinal Directions. Philosophically, the continuous looping of the lines without a clear beginning or end signifies eternity, endless fortune, and the pervasive nature of *Qi* (energy).
