About the Project

Intersecting Ethnocomputing, Culture, and Design

The Practicum Vision

Mapping Chinese Cultural Motifs originated as a design-based practicum project aimed at exploring the intersection of cultural traditions and computational thinking. Inspired by the Culturally Situated Design Tools (CSDT) framework—a pedagogical approach rooted in ethnocomputing—this project seeks to demonstrate that complex mathematical and algorithmic ideas have existed within indigenous and traditional arts for centuries.

Rather than viewing Chinese motifs simply as decorative "folk art," this curriculum treats them as sophisticated visual systems. By translating the geographic origins and geometric rules of motifs like the Ruyi Cloud into an interactive digital space, the project transforms traditional heritage into a live, explorable syllabus.

Challenging Hegemony

A driving force behind this platform is the need to combat persistent stereotypes surrounding Asian and Asian American communities, particularly the "Model Minority" myth and the "Bamboo Ceiling." In many Western and tech-centric spaces, Asian individuals are frequently stereotyped as highly technical and hard-working, but lacking in creative agency or innovative leadership.

By archiving and mapping the computational logic embedded within Chinese cultural motifs, this project acts as a counter-narrative. It reclaims the intellectual labor of early artisans—the original "programmers" of textiles, ceramics, and architecture—and proves that extreme technical rigor and profound creative expression are deeply entwined in Eastern heritage.

About the Developer

Xinyue Zhou

I am an educational designer, developer, and researcher passionate about bridging the gap between cultural heritage and computer science education. My work focuses on creating digital environments that empower students to see their own identities reflected in STEM fields.

Through this practicum, I hope to provide educators and learners with a tool that not only teaches algorithmic thinking but also fosters a deep, anti-racist appreciation for the diverse and multi-ethnic origins of global technology.
